About

I study ageing-related oral diseases through AI, multi-species experimental systems, and translational biomedicine.

Dr. Ruixue Ai works across oral medicine, ageing biology, and artificial intelligence. Her research has connected NAD+ metabolism, RNA alternative splicing, mitochondrial homeostasis, and mitophagy in ageing-related disease mechanisms.

Building on doctoral and postdoctoral work at the University of Oslo and clinical training in stomatology, her current direction focuses on intelligent diagnosis and treatment for oral potentially malignant disorders (OPMD), integrating multimodal clinical data, omics, causal inference, large language models, and AI-driven drug screening.

Research Interests

Ageing mechanisms in oral disease

Mechanistic studies of how NAD+ metabolism, RNA alternative splicing, and organelle function shape OPMD progression, malignant transformation risk, and treatment response.

AI-enabled protein isoform analysis

Deep-learning methods for protein isoform structure, interaction prediction, and cross-species validation, including PromptProtein and knowledge-graph-based reasoning.

Few-shot intelligent drug screening

AI screening methods for new mitochondrial and mitophagy targets, supported by cell, worm, mouse, and organoid validation workflows.

Multimodal intelligent diagnosis

OPMD cohorts that combine oral images, pathology, omics, structured clinical variables, and clinical text to support molecular subtyping, prognosis, and dynamic treatment recommendation.
